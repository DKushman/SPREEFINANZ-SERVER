What should you pay attention to?

The best health insurance for expats is not just cheap, but must suit your country of residence, your length of stay and your actual needs. This is exactly where most mistakes happen.

01 Scope and length of stay

Not every health insurance policy for expats is valid worldwide without restrictions. Pay attention to which countries your protection applies, whether regions such as the USA or Canada are included and how long you can remain insured. This is particularly crucial for longer stays abroad or a possible extension.

02 Services in an emergency

What is important is not just the contribution, but above all what is actually covered in the event of a claim: outpatient treatment, inpatient care, medication, diagnostics, emergency transport and, if necessary, medically sensible repatriation. Good tariffs for expats avoid expensive gaps here.

03 Pre-existing conditions, deductible and return

Many expats overlook the fact that pre-existing conditions, exclusions or high deductibles can later make the plan unattractive. Equally important: What happens when you return to Germany or change your country of residence? These points should be carefully checked from the start.

What mistakes can you make when choosing?

When choosing health insurance for expats, every detail counts. Anyone who only compares superficially risks high costs and unnecessary stress abroad.

Just look at the price

A cheap tariff is not automatically a good tariff. Many seemingly cheap solutions save precisely on the crucial points: benefit levels, return transport, pre-existing illnesses or regional restrictions. For expats, real protection is more important than the lowest monthly premium.

Misjudge the country of residence

Medical costs vary greatly depending on the country. If you live or travel in expensive regions, you need a tariff that is really designed for this. If the land is specified incorrectly or not planned properly, it can be expensive later.

Don't think about returning to Germany

Many expats worry about starting abroad, but not about exiting the tariff or returning. If you don't think ahead, you'll later have problems with transitions, deadlines or connecting to other insurance solutions.

Your most frequently asked questions

Here you will find the most important answers about health insurance for expats - understandable and reduced to the essentials.

What is health insurance for expats?

Health insurance for expats is insurance for people who live or work abroad for a long period of time. It usually goes beyond traditional travel health insurance and is designed for international stays.

Is normal international health insurance enough?

Often yes for short trips – usually not for longer stays. Expats often need an international solution with an expanded scope of services, a longer term and better adaptation to the actual center of their lives.

What should a good health insurance policy for expats cover?

Important items include outpatient and inpatient treatment, medication, diagnostics, emergency care and – depending on the situation – repatriation, preventive services or protection in several countries.

Does my protection also apply if I change country?

That depends on the tariff. Some policies are valid worldwide, others only in certain regions or with restrictions. Anyone who travels or moves flexibly as an expat should definitely check this point in advance.

How do I find the right tariff for me?

The decisive factors are your country of residence, your planned duration, your professional status, your state of health and your desired scope of services. That's exactly why it's worth getting personal advice so that you don't choose just any health insurance for expats, but rather the right one.
