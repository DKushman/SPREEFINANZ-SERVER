What should you pay attention to?

When it comes to health insurance for digital nomads, it's not just about the price. What matters is that the tariff fits your travel behavior, your length of stay and your actual risk.

01 Worldwide protection and flexible term

Digital nomads rarely stay in one place for long. That's why your health insurance should be valid in several countries and be able to react flexibly to longer trips, spontaneous extensions or changes of country. Particularly important: Check whether expensive regions such as the USA or Canada are included or treated separately.

02 Meaningful benefits instead of minimal insurance

A cheap policy is of little help to you if important costs are not covered in an emergency. Pay attention to solid services for outpatient treatment, inpatient stays, medication, diagnostics, emergency care and medically sensible repatriation. Especially if you have a mobile lifestyle, services abroad must be reliable.

03 Pre-existing illnesses, deductible and return to Germany

Anyone traveling long-term should not only think about the trip itself, but also about the transition back. Clarify in advance how previous illnesses, deductibles, interruptions and a later return to Germany will be dealt with. This way you avoid gaps in your insurance coverage.

What mistakes can you make when choosing?

Many digital nomads choose their insurance too quickly - and only later realize that crucial points don't fit. You should avoid these mistakes.

Confusing normal travel health insurance with a long-term solution

For a short vacation, simple travel health insurance may be sufficient. However, anyone who lives independently of location for months or permanently usually needs much more flexible and comprehensive health insurance for digital nomads. Otherwise, gaps will quickly arise.

Buy too cheaply and save at the wrong end

The cheapest tariff is often not the best. Important services are often missing, reimbursement is limited or certain countries and treatments are excluded. Abroad, this can quickly lead to high out-of-pocket costs.

Not thinking about changing countries and the reality of life

Many tariffs seem suitable at first glance, but are not designed for everyday mobile use. If you, as a digital nomad, regularly change countries, work remotely or spontaneously extend your trip, your insurance must be designed for exactly this.

Your most frequently asked questions

Here you will find the most important answers about health insurance for digital nomads - clear, understandable and reduced to the essentials.

What is health insurance for digital nomads?

This is health insurance for people who work remotely and stay abroad for a longer period of time. It is generally more flexible than classic travel health insurance and is better tailored to mobile living between several countries.

Is normal international health insurance enough?

Often yes for short trips – usually not for digital nomads. Anyone traveling long-term often needs a solution with a longer term, a better scope of services and more flexibility when changing countries and extending their stay.

What should a good health insurance policy for digital nomads cover?

What is particularly important are outpatient and inpatient treatments, medication, diagnostics, emergencies and - depending on the tariff - medically sensible return transport. Depending on your lifestyle, other building blocks may also make sense.

Can I also use the tariff if I change countries frequently?

Yes, but only if the tariff is designed for this. That's exactly why you should check in which countries your protection applies and whether there are regional restrictions or special regulations before taking out the contract.

How do I find the right solution for my lifestyle?

The decisive factors are your travel behavior, your length of stay, your target regions, your professional status and your desired scope of services. Personal advice will help you choose exactly the health insurance for digital nomads that really suits you.
